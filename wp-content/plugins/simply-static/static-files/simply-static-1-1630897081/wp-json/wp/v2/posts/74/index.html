Imagery from NOAA Satellites<\/a> shows the way smoke<\/a> is spreading from California<\/a> wildfires<\/a>.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The agency shared the images, in GIF format, on Twitter Tuesday.<\/p>\n

CALIFORNIA FIREFIGHTERS USE SNOW MACHINES TO FIGHT CALDOR FIRE AT TAHOE RESORT<\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

The first post showed the now-204,390-acre Caldor Fire<\/a> near Lake Tahoe<\/a> on Monday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

“The raging #CaldorFire continues to threaten communities near #LakeTahoe, seen here last evening from @NOAA’s #GOES17. At last report, the #wildfire had grown to 186,500+ acres and the governors of Calif. and Nev. have declared states of emergency,” NOAA Satellites wrote.<\/p>\n

The Caldor Fire was 20% contained on Wednesday but windy conditions in the area were expected to fan the flames as some residents in neighboring Nevada<\/a> were ordered to evacuate<\/a>.\u00a0<\/p>\n

About 22,000\u00a0residents of the city of South Lake Tahoe evacuated as well on Tuesday and officials said only a few residents defied the evacuation order.<\/p>\n

The fire, which started on Aug. 14, has destroyed more than 600 structures and threatened tens of thousands more.<\/p>\n

CALDOR FIRE MOVES CLOSER TO CALIFORNIA’S SOUTH LAKE TAHOE, FORCING MORE EVACUATION<\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

More than 15,000 firefighters were battling dozens of California fires, with assistance from crews from out of state.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The National Interagency Fire Center (NIFC) reported Wednesday<\/a> that there were 15 active large fires in the Golden State.<\/p>\n

“The Caldor Fire continues to burn actively and thousands of residents have been evacuated. The Dixie Fire displayed extreme fire behavior and made a 42,000-acre run. Several other large fires in California, Minnesota, Oregon and Washington\u00a0have forced the evacuation of nearby residents,” the fire center said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

On Monday, the U.S. Forest Service<\/a> announced that all national forest in California would be closed until Sept. 17.<\/p>\n

“From 22,300 miles up, @NOAA’s #GOES17 saw the full extent of the #wildfires blazing across northern California yesterday\u2014including the #CaldorFire, #DixieFire and others. Officials said all California national forests will temporarily close starting tonight,” NOAA Satellites said in a second tweet.<\/p>\n

According to the NIFC, 86 large fires currently burning across the country have scorched more than 2.6 million acres in 11 states.<\/p>\n

Climate change<\/a> has made the western U.S. warmer and drier and scientists say its impact will continue to make wildfires more frequent and destructive.\u00a0<\/p>\n
