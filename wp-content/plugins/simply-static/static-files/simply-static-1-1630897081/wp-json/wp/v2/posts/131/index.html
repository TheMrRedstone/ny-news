Barry Williams will always be a big brother to this TV family.<\/p>\n

The former child star famously starred as Greg Brady in the beloved sitcom<\/a> “The Brady Bunch,”<\/a> which aired from 1969 until 1974. The 66-year-old, who now lives in Branson, Missouri, recently told Closer Weekly<\/a> that he still remains close with the surviving cast.<\/p>\n

“We have a very unique experience in common,” the actor explained. “We grew up with the show playing family members and went through all of the changes in our growth in front of the world. That created some unique and sometimes challenging circumstances, but we protected each other and took care of each other. That commonality puts us in a very small club.”<\/p>\n

Williams added that he\u2019s especially close to Christopher Knight who played Peter Brady in the series.<\/p>\n

“I\u2019m very proud to have been a part of this show,” said Williams. “I\u2019m proud to have had a part in creating a character in a show that has had this long-lasting saturation in Americana.”<\/p>\n

And Williams insisted that he never gets tired of talking about “The Brady Bunch.”<\/p>\n

“No!” he said. “Sometimes I get tired of talking about myself though. \u2018The Brady Bunch\u2019 has been an integral part of my life. It\u2019s a show that has been with me now for over 50 years, and I consider the cast members a second family. I know that there is interest and curiosity around the show, and I\u2019m happy to oblige.”<\/p>\n

And despite growing up in the public eye, Williams said he\u2019s always had a deep appreciation for performing, as well as for the opportunities that Hollywood provided along the way.<\/p>\n

“Oh, I was committed to a lifetime of show business long before \u2018The Brady Bunch,\u2019” said Williams. “In fact, I don\u2019t think any of us really held up high hopes that it would have this kind of endurance. Even by television standards, it\u2019s a pretty unique series. It\u2019s been a sitcom, a cartoon show, a variety hour, a Christmas special, a movie twice (we did my book [\u2018Growing Up Brady\u2019] as a TV movie) and \u2018A Very Brady Renovation.\u2019 There have been so many things around it, more than pretty much any television series I can think of.”<\/p>\n

“No, I\u2019m grateful,” he continued. “I\u2019ve been able to have a successful career in virtually every aspect of show business \u2014 television, movies, cartoon shows, and as a musician, singer and recording artist. I have worked in Las Vegas. I\u2019ve been a DJ for SiriusXM. I have produced television. I have written a best-selling book. I think being able to have that kind of diversity has been just marvelous. There\u2019s been no boredom.”<\/p>\n

And Williams has pursued his other passions along the way. According to the outlet, the star became a private pilot at one point and flew a small single-engine aircraft “around the country.” Maui, he said, is his favorite island to visit.<\/p>\n

When asked if his two children have been compelled to pursue a similar career path, Williams said “they have not.”<\/p>\n

“My son is a musician, but also a full-time student, and hasn\u2019t made any [comments] about going into that as a profession,” he shared. “My little girl is a natural. She\u2019s got a big personality, but I am not sure she would choose [showbiz] as a profession, either.”<\/p>\n

Williams shared that he “would be 100 percent supportive” if they decided to try showbiz \u2013 or anything else.<\/p>\n

As for himself, Williams told the outlet he has zero plans to slow down.<\/p>\n

“No chance,” he said about retirement. “I am as passionate about what I do now as I was as a teenager. I\u2019m so grateful that I was able to find something that was enjoyable and satisfying for me that I still have tremendous enthusiasm and ambition for. I wouldn\u2019t think of ever retiring completely.”<\/p>\n
