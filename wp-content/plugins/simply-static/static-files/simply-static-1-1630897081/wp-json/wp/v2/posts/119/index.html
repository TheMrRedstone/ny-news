Legendary basketball player Dennis Rodman was a star on the court for the Detroit Pistons<\/a> and Chicago Bulls<\/a> during his playing days in the 90s, however, the five-time NBA champion isn\u2019t much of a fan of the league that helped him gain his fame.<\/p>\n

During Rodman\u2019s era, the NBA was known for its toughness. During a recent interview on the “Full Send<\/u><\/a>” podcast, Rodman says the league is hard to watch because of how much it has changed.<\/p>\n

“I don\u2019t know, it\u2019s just very hard to watch because once you\u2019ve played the game the way we played it intensity, just competitiveness,” Rodman said via Clutch Points<\/u><\/a>. “But now it\u2019s more like you know I don\u2019t want to watch players coming down shooting 50 footers, you know. That\u2019s not basketball.”<\/p>\n

Golden State Warriors<\/a> guard Steph Curry<\/a> and other three-point shooters have transformed a league that was built on physicality in the paint into a new era where teams now live-and-die by 3-pointers. Rodman, who was a key figure in Michael Jordan\u2019s documentary “The Last Dance”, added that the NBA is “difficult to watch, but a lot of kids love it.”<\/p>\n
